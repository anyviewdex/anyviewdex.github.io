Abstract

Vision-based dexterous manipulation policies typically assume fixed camera viewpoints, heavily restricting their deployment in unstructured real-world environments. While multi-view contrastive learning can yield view-invariant representations, applying it end-to-end within reinforcement learning often leads to training instability and a loss of fine-grained spatial precision. Consequently, existing frameworks frequently rely on explicit depth sensing to resolve the resulting 3D spatial ambiguities. In this paper, we propose a representation learning framework that achieves robust, view-invariant dexterous grasping using monocular RGB inputs.

Our approach decouples control acquisition from representation learning through an asymmetric student-teacher paradigm. We distill a state-privileged expert policy into a visual student network, regularizing a multi-view contrastive objective with an auxiliary geometric loss. This auxiliary geometric grounding anchors the visual latent space, preventing spatial collapse and implicitly injecting 3D spatial priors without requiring depth inputs. Extensive evaluations demonstrate that our framework overcomes the sample inefficiency of end-to-end visual RL. Furthermore, we achieve zero-shot sim-to-real transfer for dexterous grasping across a diverse set of objects under significant and dynamic camera perturbations.
